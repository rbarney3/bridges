Beam Bridge

What is it? A simple bridge with a flat beam across supports.

Why use it? Beam bridges are great for short spans like highways and small rivers because they are simple and cost-effective to build.

Arch Bridge

What is it? A bridge with a curved arch that carries weight outward.

Why use it? Arch bridges are strong and durable, often used to cross valleys or rivers. Their shape helps spread out the weight.

Suspension Bridge

What is it? A bridge with the deck hanging from cables held by tall towers.

Why use it? Suspension bridges can span very long distances, like wide bays or large rivers, and are flexible enough to handle wind and traffic.
